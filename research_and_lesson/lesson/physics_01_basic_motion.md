# Physics 01: Basic Motion

## Objective

Understand what longitudinal motion means and identify the main quantities used to describe it.

## Core Idea

Longitudinal vehicle dynamics studies motion along the forward-backward direction of the car. In this topic, the vehicle is treated as moving in a straight line, so the main variables are:
- position
- velocity
- acceleration

If the car speeds up, acceleration is positive. If it slows down, acceleration is negative. If the speed stays constant, acceleration is zero.

## Key Concepts

- Position tells where the vehicle is along the road.
- Velocity tells how fast the vehicle is moving.
- Acceleration tells how quickly velocity changes.
- Braking is negative longitudinal acceleration.
- Cruising means velocity is nearly constant.

## Why It Matters

This is the foundation for all later modeling. Before adding tire slip, drag, or grade, we must first define what motion is being described.

## Example

If a car increases speed from 10 m/s to 20 m/s, it has undergone positive acceleration. If it slows from 20 m/s to 5 m/s, it has undergone negative acceleration.

## Example Problems

### Problem 1

A vehicle moves straight ahead at a constant speed of 15 m/s. Is its acceleration positive, negative, or zero?

Answer:
Its acceleration is zero because the velocity is not changing.

### Problem 2

A car slows down from 25 m/s to 10 m/s. What kind of longitudinal acceleration is this?

Answer:
This is negative longitudinal acceleration because the vehicle speed decreases.
